## Abstract

Evaluation of different recruitment approaches for engaging underserved transit riders in smartphone-based data collection, finding that transit-based advertisements proved both more effective and cost-efficient than traditional methods.

## Key Findings

- Transit-based ads significantly outperformed mailings and in-person outreach
- More cost-effective recruitment for hard-to-reach populations
- Important implications for equitable research participation
- Demonstrates importance of meeting participants where they are

## Context

This work is part of the BALTO project, which developed ethical and sustainable data collection methods for equitable public transit research.
